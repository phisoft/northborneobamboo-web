Bamboo in Architecture

From the megastructures of the Vo Throng Nghia Architecture to the more modest building structure of the Bali School, the green steel known as bamboo has been incorporated into modern architecture for its’ aesthetic and many other benefits.

Natural Conductor

The wood cools the atmosphere as is known as a natural conductor, absorbing the heat whilst cooling the surrounding environment and is known to withstand the tests of time.

Higher Specific Compressive Strength

There is a scientific reasoning behind why bamboo is the choice material for many; it has a higher specific compressive strength compared to wood, brick and even concrete.

Easy to be found

While many western countries seek to import the wood, we lucky few who are close to the equator where the bamboo so easily grows have the good fortune of having an abundance of the plant growing in our backyard.

Availability

Due to the rapid growth of the plant, bamboos are the popular choice in wood because of its’ availability, among many other reasons.

Modern architecture & Technology

The boon of modern technology increased the longevity of bamboo plants with treatments, making it more than suitable for a long-lasting, durable and beautiful home.

With the shake that Sabah experienced in 2015, durability has since come to play as well. Bamboos are known for their flexibility, even after processing. While it hasn’t been a large question, it still beckons what will become of our buildings. Better to be safe than sorry!
